## What is Brand Voice?

Brand voice is the set of instructions that tells the AI how to write — the tone, personality, style preferences, and constraints that make content sound like it comes from your brand rather than a generic source.

Without brand voice configuration, the AI generates technically correct content in a neutral, general-purpose style. With it, output can sound casual or formal, data-driven or narrative, direct or explanatory — whatever fits your brand identity.

## Where Brand Voice Settings Live

Brand voice is configured at the project level. Navigate to:

**Project Settings > Brand Voice**

Each project has its own independent brand voice configuration. If you manage multiple brands or clients, configure each project's voice separately.

## Configuring Brand Voice

### Tone
Select from preset tone options or describe your own:

- **Professional** — Formal, authoritative, business-appropriate
- **Conversational** — Friendly, approachable, plain-language
- **Expert** — Technical depth, assumes knowledgeable readers
- **Inspirational** — Motivating, aspirational, emotionally resonant

You can also write a custom tone description in the free-text field, for example: "Confident but approachable. Avoids jargon. Uses clear, short sentences. Occasionally uses humor where appropriate."

### Writing Style
Controls the structural and syntactic characteristics of the output:

- **List usage** — Whether the AI should use bullet points and numbered lists frequently, sparingly, or never
- **Sentence length** — Short and punchy versus longer, more complex sentences
- **Use of passive voice** — Allow freely, minimize, or avoid entirely
- **Heading style** — Question-format headings vs. declarative headings

### Voice Characteristics
Open-ended fields where you describe your brand's character:

- **Personality traits** — "We are curious, direct, and slightly irreverent"
- **What we avoid** — "Never use corporate buzzwords like 'synergy' or 'leverage'. Avoid excessive superlatives."
- **Who we write for** — "Our readers are small business owners with limited technical background"

### Custom Instructions
A free-text field for any instructions that do not fit the structured fields above. This is the most flexible part of brand voice configuration. Examples:

- "Always refer to customers as 'members', not 'users' or 'customers'"
- "When discussing competitors, be neutral and factual — do not make comparative claims"
- "End every article with a call to action that links to our free trial"
- "Cite specific data points when available rather than using vague quantifiers like 'many' or 'most'"

> **Tip:** The custom instructions field is the highest-leverage part of brand voice setup. Start by listing 5–10 specific rules that writers on your team follow, then translate them into plain-language instructions here.

## How Brand Voice Affects AI Output

When you generate an outline or article, the brand voice settings for the current project are included in the AI prompt automatically. You do not need to add them manually to each request.

The AI applies voice settings to:
- Article body text
- Section headings and subheadings
- Introductions and conclusions
- Meta description suggestions

Voice settings do not affect image generation prompts or Knowledge Vault query responses.

## Example: Before and After Brand Voice

**Without brand voice (neutral AI output):**
> "Content marketing is a strategic marketing approach focused on creating and distributing valuable, relevant, and consistent content to attract and retain a clearly defined audience."

**With brand voice (configured for a direct, jargon-free SaaS audience):**
> "Content marketing means publishing useful content your audience actually wants to read — not ads, not fluff. The goal is to earn their trust before they ever buy from you."

Both are accurate. The second sounds like it comes from a specific brand with a point of view.

## Testing Brand Voice

After configuring brand voice, generate a short test article on a familiar topic and review the output. Ask yourself:

- Does the tone match how your team naturally writes?
- Are there recurring phrases or structures that feel off?
- Does the AI avoid the things you told it to avoid?

Refine the settings iteratively. Most brand voice configurations improve significantly after two or three rounds of testing and adjustment.

> **Tip:** Share the brand voice settings page with a writer on your team and ask them to review it. They often catch mismatches between the AI instructions and the actual writing style guide faster than anyone else.

## Sharing Voice Settings Across Projects

Brand voice is not shared between projects. If you have a common style that applies to several projects, you will need to copy the settings manually. Consider keeping a master document of your brand voice configuration outside the platform so you can replicate it quickly when creating new projects.
