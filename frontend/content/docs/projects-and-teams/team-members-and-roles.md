## Team Collaboration Overview

Projects support multiple team members working together. Each member is assigned a role that controls what actions they can take within the project. You can invite colleagues, freelancers, or clients and give each person exactly the level of access they need.

## The Four Roles

### Owner
The owner has full, unrestricted access to the project. There is one owner per project. The owner can:
- Do everything an Admin can do
- Delete the project permanently
- Transfer ownership to another member
- Cannot be removed by any other role

Ownership is automatically assigned to the person who created the project.

### Admin
Admins have nearly full access. They are intended for trusted senior team members who need to manage the project's configuration. Admins can:
- Create, edit, publish, and delete all content (articles, outlines, images)
- Manage integrations (WordPress, GSC, social accounts)
- Invite and remove members (except the owner)
- Change member roles (cannot promote to owner)
- Configure brand voice and project settings

### Editor
Editors have access to all content creation features but cannot change project configuration. This is the right role for writers, content strategists, and designers who need to do their job without touching settings. Editors can:
- Create, edit, and publish articles and outlines
- Generate AI content
- Upload to the Knowledge Vault
- Generate and manage images
- Schedule social media posts

Editors cannot manage integrations, invite other members, or change project settings.

### Viewer
Viewers have read-only access. Use this role for clients, stakeholders, or anyone who needs to review work without making changes. Viewers can:
- Read articles and outlines
- View analytics and reports
- Browse the image library
- Cannot create, edit, or delete any content

> **Tip:** Use the Viewer role for client contacts who want visibility into content progress without the risk of accidental edits.

## Inviting a Team Member

1. Navigate to your project's **Settings** page.
2. Click the **Team** or **Members** tab.
3. Click **Invite Member**.
4. Enter the person's email address.
5. Select the role you want to assign them.
6. Click **Send Invitation**.

An email invitation is sent to the address you entered. The recipient clicks the link in the email to accept the invitation and join the project. If they do not already have an account, they will be prompted to create one first.

Invitations expire after **7 days**. If the recipient does not accept in time, you can resend the invitation from the pending invitations list.

## Viewing Pending Invitations

The Members tab shows two sections: active members and pending invitations. Pending invitations display the invited email, the assigned role, and the date the invitation was sent. You can cancel a pending invitation if you need to revoke access before it is accepted.

## Changing a Member's Role

1. Open **Settings > Team**.
2. Find the member in the active members list.
3. Click the role dropdown next to their name.
4. Select the new role.
5. The change takes effect immediately — no confirmation email is sent.

You can change any member's role as long as your own role is equal to or higher than the role you are assigning. Editors and Viewers cannot change roles. Admins can change roles but cannot assign the Owner role.

## Removing a Member

1. Open **Settings > Team**.
2. Find the member you want to remove.
3. Click the remove (trash) icon next to their name.
4. Confirm the removal.

The removed member immediately loses access to the project. Their contributed content (articles, outlines) remains in the project and is not deleted.

The project Owner cannot be removed by any other member. Ownership must be transferred before the current owner can be removed.

## Leaving a Project

If you are a member (not the owner) and want to leave a project:

1. Open **Settings > Team**.
2. Scroll to the bottom of the page.
3. Click **Leave Project**.
4. Confirm.

If you are the owner and want to leave, you must first transfer ownership to another Admin-level member.

## Transferring Ownership

1. Open **Settings > Team**.
2. Click **Transfer Ownership** (available only to the current owner).
3. Select the Admin-level member who will become the new owner.
4. Confirm the transfer.

After transfer, your role becomes Admin. You retain access to the project but can no longer perform owner-only actions.

> **Tip:** Before offboarding from a project you own, always transfer ownership rather than simply abandoning the project. Ownerless projects cannot be fully managed by the remaining team.
