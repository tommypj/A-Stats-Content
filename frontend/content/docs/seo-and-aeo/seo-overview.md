## SEO Scoring That Works While You Write

Most SEO tools evaluate your content after publication — by then, you're reacting to problems instead of preventing them. A-Stats scores your content in real time across ten ranking factors, updating as you type.

---

## What Gets Scored

| Factor | What It Measures |
|---|---|
| **Keyword Density** | Whether your primary keyword appears at the right frequency |
| **Title Optimization** | Keyword placement, length, and clarity of your page title |
| **Meta Description** | Presence, length, and keyword inclusion |
| **Heading Structure** | Proper H1–H4 hierarchy with logical flow |
| **Internal Linking** | Links to other pages on your site |
| **Readability** | Sentence complexity, paragraph length, reading ease |
| **Content Length** | Whether the article meets competitive depth for its keyword |
| **Image Optimization** | Alt text presence and descriptive quality |
| **URL Structure** | Clean, keyword-relevant slug |
| **Content Freshness** | Signals that the content is current |

Each factor contributes to a composite score. Individual breakdowns show exactly where to focus.

---

## Why Real-Time Matters

Traditional SEO audits run on a publish-then-fix cycle: write, publish, audit, find issues, revise, republish. That loop takes hours or days.

With A-Stats, you see the impact of every edit instantly. Keyword density drops mid-draft — you see it. Heading hierarchy breaks — the score reflects it before you finish the paragraph. Content ships optimized on the first publish, not the third revision.

---

## Built for Writers

The scoring interface is designed to be useful without deep SEO expertise. Each factor includes a plain-language explanation of what it measures and why it matters. A content writer can produce well-optimized articles without consulting an SEO specialist.

AI-generated articles come pre-optimized against these same ten factors, giving you a strong starting score to refine.

> **Tip:** Raising a low-scoring factor by a point or two often has more impact than perfecting one that already scores well.
