## What is AEO?

Search is changing. When someone asks a question today, the answer increasingly comes not from a list of blue links but from an AI-generated summary --- a paragraph synthesized by ChatGPT, Google AI Overviews, Perplexity, or Bing Copilot. The content those engines choose to cite determines which brands get visibility in this new landscape.

Answer Engine Optimization (AEO) is the practice of structuring your content so that AI systems are more likely to select it as a source. A-Stats is one of the first platforms to score content for AEO alongside traditional SEO, in a single unified editor.

### Why AEO Matters Now

AI-powered search is not a future trend. It is the current reality for a growing share of queries:

- **Google AI Overviews** appear above organic results for informational queries, often reducing click-through to the underlying pages.
- **ChatGPT with browsing** and **Perplexity** directly cite web sources in their responses, sending traffic to the pages they reference.
- **Bing Copilot** synthesizes answers from indexed content and attributes sources inline.

Content that is not optimized for these systems risks becoming invisible --- even if it ranks well in traditional search. AEO is the discipline that addresses this gap.

### How AI Engines Select Content to Cite

AI citation is not random. These systems favor content that exhibits specific structural and substantive qualities:

| Quality | Why It Matters |
|---|---|
| **Direct Answer Clarity** | Content that states answers concisely near the top of a section is more likely to be extracted |
| **Structural Clarity** | Clean heading hierarchies, numbered lists, and definition patterns make content easier for AI to parse |
| **FAQ Sections** | Explicit question-and-answer formatting maps directly to the query patterns AI engines process |
| **Factual Density** | Content rich in specific data points, statistics, and verifiable claims is preferred over vague commentary |
| **Topical Authority** | Pages that demonstrate comprehensive coverage of a subject signal reliability to AI systems |

These are not the same signals that drive traditional search ranking. A page can rank first on Google organically yet never be cited by an AI overview because it lacks the structural cues those systems look for.

### SEO vs. AEO: Different Goals, One Editor

SEO optimizes for search engine ranking --- getting your page onto the first page of results. AEO optimizes for AI citation --- getting your content selected as a source in generated answers.

The two disciplines overlap but diverge in important ways:

| | SEO | AEO |
|---|---|---|
| **Goal** | Rank higher in search results | Get cited by AI-generated answers |
| **Key signals** | Keywords, backlinks, technical health | Answer clarity, structure, factual density |
| **Feedback cycle** | Days to weeks (crawl + reindex) | Immediate (content structure is the signal) |
| **Visibility** | Blue link in a results page | Named source in an AI response |

A-Stats scores both simultaneously. As you write, you see your SEO score and your AEO score side by side. This dual scoring ensures your content is competitive in both traditional and AI-driven search without requiring two separate optimization passes.

### How A-Stats Scores AEO

The AEO scoring engine evaluates your content against the five citation factors listed above. Each factor is scored individually, and the composite AEO score reflects how likely your content is to be selected as a source by major AI engines.

Like the SEO score, the AEO score updates in real time as you edit. Add an FAQ section and watch the score adjust. Tighten a vague paragraph into a concrete, data-backed statement and see the factual density factor respond.

> **Tip:** The fastest way to improve an AEO score is to ensure every major section begins with a clear, direct answer to the question implied by its heading. AI engines extract these lead-in sentences disproportionately often.
