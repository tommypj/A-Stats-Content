## What Is the Content Calendar?

The content calendar gives you a visual, date-based view of everything you are planning and publishing. Rather than working through a flat list of articles and outlines, you can see the full publishing schedule laid out across weeks and months, spot gaps in your cadence, and move items around by dragging them to a new date.

The calendar is not a publishing scheduler in the sense of auto-posting at a specific time — it is a planning layer that sits alongside your articles and social media queue, giving you a single place to coordinate content across your team and projects.

---

## Opening the Calendar

Go to **Content → Calendar** in the sidebar. The calendar opens in the current month by default. Use the **<** and **>** arrows to move between months, or click **Today** to return to the present.

You can switch between three view modes using the tabs at the top right:

- **Month** — The default. Best for a high-level overview of the full publishing schedule.
- **Week** — Shows a 7-day band with more detail per day. Useful when you are finalizing content for the coming week.
- **List** — A flat, date-sorted list of all scheduled items. Best for export or sharing with stakeholders.

---

## What Appears on the Calendar

Each item on the calendar represents one of the following:

| Item Type | When It Appears |
|---|---|
| **Article (Draft)** | When the article has a scheduled publish date set. |
| **Article (Published)** | On its actual publish date (or the date it was marked published). |
| **Outline** | When a target date has been set on the outline. |
| **Social post** | When a social post has a scheduled send time. |

Items that have no date set do not appear on the calendar. To add an article or outline to the calendar, open it and set its **Target Date** or **Scheduled Date** field.

---

## Status Colors

Calendar items are color-coded by status so you can read the schedule at a glance.

| Color | Status |
|---|---|
| Gray | Outline (not yet generated) |
| Blue | Article — Draft |
| Green | Article — Published |
| Yellow | Article — Needs Review |
| Purple | Social post — Scheduled |
| Red | Overdue (target date has passed, item not published) |

> **Tip:** Red items on the calendar are a quick way to catch content that slipped. Use the List view to export overdue items and triage them as a weekly habit.

---

## Planning Content Ahead

### Setting a Target Date on an Outline

Open any outline and find the **Target Date** field in the right-hand settings panel. Setting this date adds the outline to the calendar as a planning placeholder. When the article is eventually generated and assigned a publish date, the outline placeholder is replaced by the article item.

### Setting a Publish Date on an Article

Open any article in the editor. In the right-hand panel, under **Publishing**, set the **Scheduled Date**. The article immediately appears on the calendar on that date.

If you publish the article to WordPress, the platform records the actual publish date automatically, and the article's calendar item moves to that date if it differs from the scheduled date.

> **Tip:** Build your calendar 4–6 weeks ahead. A visible forward plan makes it easier to see topic gaps, avoid keyword cannibalization across nearby publish dates, and coordinate article generation with your writing and review cycles.

---

## Moving Items

In Month and Week view, you can drag any calendar item to a different date. Dropping it on a new date updates the **Scheduled Date** field on the article or outline automatically. No additional save action is needed.

---

## Filtering by Project

If you manage multiple projects, the calendar can show all projects at once or a single project at a time.

Use the **Project** filter dropdown at the top left of the calendar to:

- Show **All Projects** — every scheduled item across your account.
- Select a specific project — narrows the calendar to only that project's content.

Project items are also distinguished by a small colored project badge on each calendar card when the **All Projects** view is active.

> **Tip:** If you are an agency user managing content for multiple clients, use the project filter to pull up a single client's calendar before a status call. The List view with one project selected is a clean, shareable snapshot of what is planned and what has shipped.

---

## Filtering by Status

Alongside the project filter, use the **Status** filter to isolate specific item types:

- **All** — Shows every item regardless of status.
- **Drafts only** — Focuses on content still being written or reviewed.
- **Published only** — Useful for reviewing what has already gone out.
- **Overdue** — Shows only items past their target or scheduled date that are not yet published.
