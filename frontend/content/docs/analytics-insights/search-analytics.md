## Search Analytics

Every SEO strategy starts with understanding how your content actually performs in search results. A-Stats pulls your Google Search Console data directly into your dashboard, giving you a unified view of keyword rankings, page-level metrics, and trend lines -- without switching between tools.

## What You Get

A-Stats connects to Google Search Console through a read-only OAuth integration. No data is modified in your GSC account. Once connected, your dashboard surfaces four core metrics for every page and keyword:

| Metric | What It Tells You |
|---|---|
| **Clicks** | How many users clicked through to your page from search results |
| **Impressions** | How often your page appeared in search results, whether clicked or not |
| **CTR** | Click-through rate -- the ratio of clicks to impressions, expressed as a percentage |
| **Average Position** | Your page's mean ranking position for a given query over the selected period |

These metrics are available at both the keyword level and the page level, so you can drill down from a broad view of your site's search presence to the performance of a single URL.

## Keyword Dashboard

The keyword dashboard aggregates all queries driving impressions and clicks to your site. Sort by any metric column to surface your strongest-performing terms, identify high-impression keywords with low CTR (optimization opportunities), or find queries where your average position is close to page one.

Filters let you narrow the view by date range, search type, and device category. Compare two date ranges side by side to see which keywords are gaining or losing ground.

## Page Performance

The page performance view flips the perspective: instead of looking at keywords, you look at individual URLs. Each page displays its aggregate search metrics along with the full list of queries it ranks for. This makes it straightforward to understand what a page is known for in Google's index and whether that aligns with your intent.

Pages are sortable by total clicks, impressions, CTR, or average position. Combined with the keyword breakdown, you can quickly spot pages that rank for valuable terms but underperform on click-through -- a signal that title tags or meta descriptions may need attention.

## Position Tracking Over Time

A-Stats records your ranking data daily, building a historical trend line for every keyword and page. Position tracking charts show movement over weeks and months so you can correlate ranking changes with content updates, link-building efforts, or algorithm shifts.

Sudden drops are easier to catch when you have a visual timeline rather than a static snapshot. Combined with A-Stats' content decay detection, position tracking gives you an early warning system before traffic loss becomes significant.

## Why It Matters

Scattered data leads to slow decisions. By centralizing your search performance alongside your content pipeline, editorial calendar, and SEO scores, A-Stats eliminates the context-switching that slows down content teams. You see what is working, what is slipping, and where to invest your next round of effort -- all from one screen.

> **Tip:** Pair search analytics with A-Stats' SEO scoring to compare your on-page optimization against real-world ranking performance. A high SEO score with a low average position often points to off-page factors worth investigating.
