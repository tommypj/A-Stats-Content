## Content Decay Detection

Publishing a great article is only half the job. Over time, even high-performing pages lose rankings, traffic, and relevance. Search algorithms evolve, competitors publish fresher material, and user intent shifts. This gradual decline is called content decay -- and most teams only notice it after the damage is done.

A-Stats automates the detection of content decay so you can act before a slow decline turns into a significant traffic loss.

## What Content Decay Looks Like

Content decay rarely happens overnight. A page that ranked third for a competitive keyword might slip to fifth over two weeks, then to the second page of results over the following month. Clicks drop, impressions may hold steady for a while (your page still appears, just lower), and eventually the article stops contributing meaningful traffic.

The challenge is that most content libraries contain dozens or hundreds of pages. Manually monitoring each one for early signs of decline is impractical. That is exactly the problem automated detection solves.

## How A-Stats Monitors for Decay

A-Stats continuously evaluates your published content against its recent performance history. The system compares key metrics on both a week-over-week and month-over-month basis:

| Signal | What Is Compared |
|---|---|
| **Position change** | Average ranking position for tracked keywords, current period vs. prior period |
| **Click decline** | Total organic clicks to the page, current period vs. prior period |
| **Impression shifts** | Changes in search impression volume that may precede click drops |
| **CTR movement** | Click-through rate changes that indicate shifting SERP competition |

When a page crosses the configured threshold for any of these signals, A-Stats flags it as decaying and surfaces it in your content health dashboard.

## Configurable Thresholds

Every site is different. A 10-position drop on a brand-new blog might be normal volatility, while a 2-position drop on your highest-revenue page could be critical. A-Stats lets you configure decay thresholds to match your tolerance for risk, so alerts reflect what actually matters to your business rather than generating noise.

## Alert System

Detection without notification is just data sitting in a dashboard. A-Stats sends email alerts when content decay is identified, so the right people on your team are informed even if they have not logged into the platform that day. Alerts include the affected page, the metric that triggered the flag, and the magnitude of the change.

> **Tip:** Combine decay alerts with A-Stats' content calendar to schedule refresh tasks immediately when decay is detected. Catching a decline within the first two weeks gives you the best chance of recovering lost positions with a targeted content update.

## Why Catching Decay Early Matters

Search engines reward freshness and relevance. A page that has been declining for three months requires significantly more effort to recover than one caught within two weeks of its first ranking drop. Early detection means smaller updates -- refreshing statistics, adding a new section, improving internal linking -- rather than full rewrites.

For teams managing large content libraries, automated decay detection transforms content maintenance from a reactive scramble into a structured, proactive workflow. You stop guessing which pages need attention and start working from a prioritized list backed by real performance data.
