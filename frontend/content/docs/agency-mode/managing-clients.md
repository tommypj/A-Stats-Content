## Client Records

In agency mode, a client record is a named entity that groups one or more projects under a single client relationship. The record stores the client's name, contact information, and branding preferences, and controls access to the client portal.

Client records are separate from platform user accounts. Your clients do not need to create accounts — they access their portal through a unique link that you control.

## Adding a Client

1. Navigate to **Agency** in the main sidebar.
2. Click **Add Client**.
3. Fill in the client details:
   - **Client Name** (required) — The name that appears in your agency dashboard and in the client portal heading
   - **Primary Contact Name** — The main person you work with at this client
   - **Primary Contact Email** — Used for portal access notifications
   - **Website URL** — The client's website, used as context for integrations
   - **Notes** — Internal notes visible only to your team
4. Click **Save Client**.

The client record is created and appears in your client list. No portal access is granted yet — that is a separate step.

## Associating Projects with a Client

After creating a client record, link the projects that belong to that client:

1. Open the client record from the agency dashboard.
2. Click **Associate Projects**.
3. Select one or more projects from the dropdown (only projects you own or admin appear here).
4. Click **Save**.

The selected projects now appear in the client's portal when they log in. A single project can be associated with only one client at a time.

> **Tip:** Create the project before creating the client record, so it is available to associate immediately. Projects must exist before they can be linked to a client.

## Client Workspace View

When you view a client record in the agency dashboard, you see a workspace summary — a consolidated view of all projects associated with that client:

- Combined article count (drafts, published, scheduled)
- Organic traffic trend across all associated projects
- Upcoming scheduled social media posts
- Pending content that requires action (articles in review, outline approvals)

This view lets you quickly assess the state of each client's content production without opening each project individually.

## Organizing Multiple Clients

### Client Status
Set a status on each client record to organize your portfolio:

- **Active** — Current, ongoing engagement
- **Paused** — Engagement temporarily on hold
- **Completed** — Engagement finished; projects are archived

The default client list view shows Active clients. Use the filter to show Paused or Completed clients.

### Search and Filter
Use the search bar at the top of the client list to find clients by name. Filter by status to see only Active, Paused, or Completed relationships.

### Sorting
Sort the client list by:
- Client name (alphabetical)
- Date added (most recent first)
- Content volume (most articles produced)
- Organic traffic (highest traffic first)

## Editing Client Details

Click into a client record and select **Edit** to update any of the client's details. Changes to the client name update how the client appears in your dashboard immediately. Changes to branding (logo, colors) take effect in the client's portal within a few minutes.

## Archiving a Client

When a client engagement ends:

1. Open the client record.
2. Click **Archive Client**.

Archiving sets the client's status to Completed and moves their record to the archived section of the client list. Their associated projects are not deleted or archived — they remain in your project list as active projects unless you archive them separately.

> **Tip:** Archive the client record when the engagement ends, then archive the individual projects separately. This way the content is preserved and accessible for future reference, but neither appears in your active working views.

## Removing a Client

To permanently delete a client record:

1. Open the client record.
2. Click **Delete Client**.
3. Confirm by typing the client name.

Deleting a client record removes the client record and revokes all portal access. It does **not** delete the associated projects or their content. Projects previously linked to this client become unlinked and remain in your standard project list.

## Client Communication

The platform does not currently support direct messaging with clients. Use the client portal to share content for review, and communicate with clients through your existing channels (email, Slack, etc.).

> **Tip:** Use the Notes field on client records to log key decisions, communication preferences, and context that should be visible to all team members who work on that client account.
