## What is Agency Mode?

Agency mode is a set of features designed for content agencies, freelancers, and consultants who manage content production for multiple clients. It extends the standard platform with client management tools, white-label branding, and a client-facing portal — all without requiring your clients to have their own platform accounts.

Agency mode is available on the **Pro** and **Enterprise** plans.

## Who Agency Mode is For

**Content agencies** managing five or more clients who each need separate content, analytics, and publishing workflows.

**Freelance content strategists** who want to deliver a professional, branded experience to clients rather than sharing a generic dashboard link.

**In-house marketing teams** at holding companies or franchises managing content for multiple brands under one organizational umbrella.

If you manage content for only one brand or client, the standard project workflow is sufficient. Agency mode adds value specifically when you need to present work to clients, manage multiple client relationships, and maintain separation between client environments.

## Key Features

### Client Workspaces
Each client gets their own workspace within your agency account. A client workspace is essentially a scoped view of one or more projects, presented under your branding. Clients can log into their portal and see their content, reports, and analytics — without seeing any of your other clients.

### White-Label Branding
Your agency's logo, colors, and name appear throughout the client-facing experience. The platform's own branding is removed from the client portal. Clients see a professional environment that looks like your agency's proprietary tool.

### Client Portal
Each client receives a unique portal link. Clicking the link takes them to a branded dashboard where they can review content, approve drafts, view SEO performance reports, and see social media results — without the ability to create or modify anything (unless you grant write access).

### Centralized Reporting
From your agency dashboard, you get a summary view across all clients: total content produced, aggregate organic traffic, pending approvals, and upcoming scheduled posts. This gives you an at-a-glance status of your entire client portfolio.

## How Agency Mode Relates to Projects

Agency mode sits on top of the standard project system. Every piece of content still lives in a project, with the same project structure you already use. Agency mode adds:

1. A **client record** that associates one or more projects with a named client.
2. A **portal** that surfaces those projects to the client in a branded interface.
3. **Branding configuration** that applies to the portal experience.

You continue to do all content creation, generation, and publishing in the standard project workflow. Agency mode handles client visibility and presentation.

## Getting Started with Agency Mode

### Step 1: Enable Agency Mode
Agency mode is available automatically on Pro and Enterprise plans. Look for the **Agency** section in the main navigation sidebar.

### Step 2: Configure Branding
Before inviting any clients, set up your white-label branding. This ensures the first thing a client sees is your branded portal, not the default platform styling. See the [White-Label Branding](./white-label-branding.md) guide for full configuration steps.

### Step 3: Create Client Records
Add each client as a named entity in the agency dashboard. Associate their projects with their client record. See [Managing Clients](./managing-clients.md) for details.

### Step 4: Enable Client Portal
Enable the portal for each client and share the portal link. Clients receive an email with access instructions. See the [Client Portal](./client-portal.md) guide for access control options.

> **Tip:** Do your branding setup before sending any portal links to clients. There is no way to retroactively brand a portal link a client has already visited — first impressions matter.

## Agency Dashboard

The agency dashboard (accessed via **Agency** in the sidebar) provides:

- A list of all clients with status indicators
- Summary metrics per client (articles published this month, organic traffic trend, pending items)
- Quick navigation into any client's projects
- Branding configuration controls
- Portal link management

This dashboard is visible only to you and your team members — not to clients.
