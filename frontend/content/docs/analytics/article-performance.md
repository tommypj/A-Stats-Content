## Individual Article Metrics

Every article in your A-Stats content library has a dedicated performance panel that surfaces real search data from Google Search Console alongside the article's internal metadata. To view it, open any article from **Content → Articles** and select the **Performance** tab.

This panel requires a connected GSC account. If GSC is not yet connected, the Performance tab will display a prompt to complete the integration. See the Connecting Google Search Console guide for setup steps.

## Metrics Available Per Article

### Clicks

The total number of times a user clicked your article from Google Search results during the selected period. This is the most direct measure of organic traffic driven by the article.

### Impressions

How many times your article appeared in search results — whether or not the user scrolled to it or clicked it. Rising impressions with flat clicks typically means you are gaining rankings but your title or meta description is not compelling enough to drive the click.

### Average Position

Your article's mean ranking position across all queries it appeared for during the period. This is averaged across all queries and all pages of results where the article appeared, so treat it as a directional signal rather than a precise rank.

### CTR (Click-Through Rate)

Clicks divided by impressions. For articles ranking in positions 1–3, a CTR of 20–35% is typical. Articles ranking 4–10 often see 3–12%. If your CTR is significantly below these benchmarks for your position, the title and meta description are the first things to improve.

## Position and Traffic Over Time

The Performance tab includes a line chart showing how clicks and average position have trended over the selected date range. Use the date picker to examine different windows — last 28 days for recent performance, last 6 months to spot longer seasonal or decay trends.

> Tip: If you see a clear inflection point where clicks dropped suddenly, check if you published an update to the article around that date, or if a Google algorithm update occurred in that window. The Google Search Central blog documents update dates.

## Comparing Articles

The **Content → Articles** list view includes inline performance columns (clicks, position, date of last update) when GSC is connected. This lets you scan your entire library for relative performance without opening individual articles.

To do a more detailed comparison, use the **Analytics → Pages** tab under the Keywords section. That view lists every URL with full metric data and lets you sort by any column to quickly surface your best and worst performers.

## Identifying Top Performers

Sort your article list by **Clicks (descending)** to find your highest-traffic articles. These pages are your most valuable content assets and deserve regular attention:

- Keep them updated with fresh information and current statistics.
- Strengthen internal links from these pages to newer articles you want to rank.
- Consider whether the topic can be expanded into a content cluster with supporting articles.

> Tip: Top performers by clicks are not always top performers by revenue. Connect revenue attribution to understand which articles actually drive conversions, not just traffic.

## Identifying Underperformers

Sort by **Impressions (descending)** then look at CTR. Articles with many impressions but very low CTR are ranking but not getting clicked — a clear signal that the title tag or meta description needs revision.

Sort by **Position (ascending, highest number first)** to find articles ranking between positions 11 and 30. These are on the cusp of page 1 and often represent your best ROI optimization opportunities — a modest improvement in ranking can multiply clicks significantly.

## The AEO Score and Search Performance

Each article also displays an **AEO (Answer Engine Optimization) score** reflecting how well the content is structured for AI answer engines and featured snippets. Articles with high AEO scores and strong GSC positions tend to attract both traditional clicks and zero-click impressions from AI-powered search results.

You can re-run the AEO analysis for any article using the **Refresh AEO Score** button on the Performance tab. This is useful after you have made structural changes to the article content.

## Exporting Performance Data

Performance data for individual articles is not currently exportable as a standalone report. For bulk data export, use the Analytics section's full page-level view, which supports table export via the options menu in the top-right corner of the data table.
