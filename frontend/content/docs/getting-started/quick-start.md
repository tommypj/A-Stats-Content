## Before You Begin

You will need an A-Stats account. If you do not have one yet, go to the A-Stats sign-up page and create a free account. No credit card is required to start — the free tier gives you enough to complete this guide.

This guide takes approximately ten minutes to complete. By the end, you will have created a project, generated an AI-written article from a keyword, reviewed its SEO score, and know where to go next.

---

## Step 1: Sign Up and Confirm Your Email

1. Go to the A-Stats sign-up page and enter your name, email, and password.
2. Check your inbox for a confirmation email and click the verification link.
3. You will be redirected to the dashboard. Your account is ready.

> **Tip:** If the confirmation email does not arrive within two minutes, check your spam folder. The sender address is `noreply@a-stats.io`.

---

## Step 2: Create Your First Project

Projects are the organizational unit in A-Stats. All your articles, outlines, knowledge documents, and analytics live inside a project.

1. From the dashboard, click **New Project** in the top-right corner.
2. Enter a project name — use your website name or topic area (e.g., "TechReview Blog" or "Client: Acme Corp").
3. Optionally, set a **brand voice**. This tells the AI how your content should sound. You can skip this now and configure it later in project settings.
4. Click **Create Project**. You will be taken into the project workspace.

> **Tip:** You can create multiple projects — one per website, client, or content vertical. Each project has its own settings, team members, and integrations.

---

## Step 3: Create an Outline

Outlines are the foundation of every article in A-Stats. An outline defines the structure — the sections the AI will expand into a full article.

1. In your project, click **Outlines** in the left sidebar.
2. Click **New Outline**.
3. Enter your target **keyword** (e.g., "best project management tools for remote teams").
4. Set a **target word count** — 1,200 words is a good starting point for a standard article.
5. Click **Generate Outline**. The AI will produce a structured outline with a suggested title and several sections.
6. Review the sections. You can drag to reorder them, click a section to rename it, add new sections, or delete ones that do not fit.
7. When you are happy with the structure, click **Save Outline**.

> **Tip:** The more specific your keyword, the more focused the outline. "project management tools" will produce a broad outline; "project management tools for remote engineering teams" will produce a more targeted one.

---

## Step 4: Generate the Article

1. From your saved outline, click **Generate Article**.
2. A-Stats sends each section to the AI and assembles the full article. This typically takes 20–40 seconds depending on article length.
3. The editor opens automatically when generation completes.

---

## Step 5: Review the SEO Score

The editor shows your **SEO Score** in the right panel. The score runs from 0 to 100 across ten checks.

1. Look at the score breakdown. Each check shows a pass or fail status with a brief explanation.
2. Common quick wins: add your target keyword to the meta description, ensure the title contains the keyword, check that the article has at least one H2 heading.
3. Edit the article content directly in the editor. The score updates as you type.

> **Tip:** Aim for a score above 70 before publishing. A score of 85+ puts you in a strong position for competitive keywords.

---

## Step 6: Review the AEO Score

Below the SEO score, you will see your **AEO Score**. This measures how well your content is structured to be cited by AI-powered search engines.

1. Check which AEO criteria are failing. Common issues: questions in the article are not answered directly, content lacks a clear summary or conclusion, or the article does not use structured list formats where appropriate.
2. Edit to address the failing checks. Adding a short direct-answer paragraph near the top of the article often has a large impact.

---

## Step 7: Make Final Edits and Save

1. Edit the article body in the rich-text editor. You can format text, add headings, insert bullet lists, and adjust the structure.
2. Fill in the **meta description** field in the right panel — this is used for SEO and is not generated automatically.
3. Click **Save**. A-Stats auto-saves periodically, but a manual save ensures nothing is lost.

> **Tip:** The editor shows a warning in your browser if you try to navigate away with unsaved changes. You will not lose work accidentally.

---

## Step 8: What to Do Next

You have completed the core workflow. Here are the next steps depending on your goals:

- **Publish to your CMS**: Copy the article HTML or markdown from the editor, or use the WordPress integration (configurable in Project Settings > Integrations) to publish directly.
- **Schedule social posts**: Go to **Social** in the sidebar to draft and schedule promotion posts for LinkedIn, X, or Facebook.
- **Connect Google Search Console**: Go to **Analytics > Search Console** and connect your GSC account to start tracking this article's search performance.
- **Build your Knowledge Vault**: Go to **Knowledge** and upload brand guidelines or reference documents to give the AI better context for future articles.
- **Invite team members**: Go to **Project Settings > Members** to add collaborators with appropriate roles.
