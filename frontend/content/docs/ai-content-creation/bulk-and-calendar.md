## Bulk Generation & Calendar

Producing one article at a time works for small operations. Scaling content production to dozens or hundreds of pieces per month requires different tooling. A-Stats provides two systems designed for volume: bulk content generation and the content calendar. Together, they let you plan, produce, and publish large content programs without losing quality control.

### Bulk Content Generation

Bulk generation lets you queue multiple articles for production in a single workflow. There are several ways to feed articles into the pipeline at scale:

**Templates** — Define reusable article templates with preset configurations: target keyword patterns, AI settings, tone, Knowledge Vault sources, and section structures. Apply a template to a batch of keywords and every article inherits the same quality standards. Templates are particularly effective for product pages, location-based content, or any content type that follows a repeatable structure.

**CSV Import** — Upload a spreadsheet of keywords and metadata to generate articles in batch. Each row becomes an article in the pipeline. CSV import supports columns for:

| Column | Purpose |
|--------|---------|
| Keyword | The primary target keyword for the article |
| Title | Optional custom title override |
| Template | Which template to apply |
| Priority | Scheduling priority in the queue |
| Notes | Editorial notes passed to the AI as additional context |

**Batch Processing** — Once your keywords are loaded, the multi-model pipeline processes them in sequence. Each article goes through the full research-outline-article workflow. You can review outlines in bulk before triggering article generation, maintaining editorial oversight without bottlenecking the process.

### The Content Calendar

The content calendar provides a visual timeline of your entire content operation. Articles move through stages — draft, in review, scheduled, published — and appear on the calendar according to their target publication date.

Key capabilities:

- **Drag-and-drop scheduling** — Move articles between dates by dragging them on the calendar view. Reschedule an entire week's content in seconds.
- **Auto-publish to WordPress** — Articles scheduled on the calendar can be set to publish automatically to your connected WordPress site at the specified date and time. The push includes the full article, featured image, and SEO metadata.
- **Pipeline status tracking** — See at a glance which articles are still generating, which are awaiting review, and which are ready for publication.
- **Multi-project views** — If you manage multiple projects or client sites, the calendar aggregates content across all of them.

### How Bulk and Calendar Work Together

The real power emerges when these systems are combined:

1. **Plan** — Map out your content strategy on the calendar. Block out publication dates for the month or quarter.
2. **Generate** — Use bulk generation to produce all the articles you need. Upload keywords via CSV or apply templates to keyword lists.
3. **Review** — Outlines appear for batch review. Approve, edit, or reject them before article generation begins.
4. **Schedule** — As articles complete, drag them onto their target dates on the calendar.
5. **Publish** — Auto-publish pushes finished articles to WordPress on schedule, complete with featured images and SEO metadata.

This workflow turns content production from a serial, manual process into a parallel, semi-automated one. A content team can plan a month of articles in an afternoon, generate them over a few days, and have them publish automatically on schedule.

### Scaling Without Sacrificing Quality

Volume does not have to mean lower standards. Several features keep quality consistent at scale:

- **Template-enforced structure** — Every article in a batch follows the same structural blueprint, ensuring consistency across large content sets.
- **Outline review gates** — Bulk generation does not skip the outline step. You still review and approve structures before articles are written.
- **SEO and AEO scoring** — Every generated article receives real-time scoring, making it easy to identify pieces that need attention before publication.
- **Knowledge Vault context** — Brand documents apply across all bulk-generated content, keeping terminology and messaging on-brand regardless of volume.

> **Tip:** Start with a small batch of 5-10 articles using a template to calibrate your settings and section descriptions. Once the output consistently meets your standards, scale up to larger batches with confidence.

### Built for Teams and Agencies

For agencies managing multiple client sites, bulk generation and the calendar integrate with A-Stats project and role system. Editors can review outlines, admins can approve publication schedules, and clients can view progress through white-label portals — all without sharing credentials or losing oversight of the content pipeline.

The role hierarchy — owner, admin, editor, viewer — ensures that the right people have the right level of access at each stage. Viewers can monitor progress. Editors can refine outlines. Admins can approve schedules and trigger publication. This layered control is what makes bulk content production viable for teams where quality cannot be optional.
