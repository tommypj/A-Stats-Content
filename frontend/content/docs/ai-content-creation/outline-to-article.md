## From Outline to Article

The outline is the most important step in AI content creation. It is the structural blueprint that determines what your article covers, in what order, and with what emphasis. A-Stats separates outline creation from article writing deliberately — giving you full editorial control over the architecture of every piece before the AI writes a single paragraph.

### Why the Two-Step Approach Matters

Most AI writing tools take a prompt and produce a finished article in one shot. The problem is obvious: if the AI misunderstands the angle, misses a key subtopic, or structures the argument poorly, you are left rewriting large sections manually.

A-Stats eliminates this by splitting the process:

1. **Outline generation** — The AI proposes a structure based on SERP research and your keyword.
2. **Your review** — You approve, modify, or rebuild the outline.
3. **Article generation** — The AI expands your approved outline into full prose.

This means every article is built on a foundation you have validated. The AI writes faster, produces more relevant content, and requires far less post-generation editing.

### What an Outline Contains

Each outline is composed of sections, and each section includes:

| Element | Description |
|---------|-------------|
| **Heading** | The H2 or H3 that will appear in the final article |
| **Section Description** | A brief directive telling the AI what to cover, what angle to take, and what points to include |
| **Order** | The position of the section within the article's flow |

The section description is where your editorial influence is strongest. A heading like "Benefits of Remote Work" is vague. A section description like "Focus on productivity data from 2024-2025 studies, address the hybrid model counterargument, conclude with implications for hiring strategy" produces a dramatically more targeted result.

### AI-Suggested vs. Manual Sections

When the AI generates an outline, it suggests sections based on its SERP analysis — what competing articles cover, which questions appear in "People Also Ask," and what topical gaps exist. These suggestions are a starting point, not a mandate.

You can:

- **Keep AI suggestions as-is** when the research aligns with your goals.
- **Edit section headings and descriptions** to shift the angle or narrow the focus.
- **Remove sections** that are irrelevant to your audience or strategy.
- **Add entirely new sections** that the AI did not suggest — covering proprietary insights, brand-specific topics, or angles competitors have missed.
- **Reorder sections** to build the narrative arc you want.

The best outlines tend to be a blend: AI-suggested structure refined with human editorial judgment.

### How the Article Expands from the Outline

Once you approve the outline, Claude Sonnet processes each section individually, expanding the heading and description into complete prose. The AI:

- Follows the section description as its primary directive for content and angle.
- Maintains consistent tone and voice based on your project's AI settings.
- Pulls relevant context from your Knowledge Vault uploads when available.
- Builds logical transitions between sections to create a cohesive reading experience.
- Integrates research data gathered during the SERP analysis stage.

Because each section has its own description, the AI has clear guidance at every point in the article. This is what prevents the common "AI drift" problem where generated content becomes repetitive or wanders off-topic in longer pieces.

### Editing Before and After Generation

The outline stage is your primary editing opportunity, but it is not your only one. A-Stats supports editing at multiple points:

- **Before generation** — Restructure the outline, rewrite section descriptions, add or remove sections. This is the highest-leverage edit you can make.
- **After generation** — Edit the finished article directly. Refine language, add proprietary data, adjust claims, or polish transitions.

The key insight is that editing an outline takes seconds. Editing a 2,000-word article takes considerably longer. By front-loading your editorial input into the outline, you minimize the post-generation work.

> **Tip:** Write section descriptions as if you are briefing a skilled writer. The more specific your instructions — mentioning data points to include, arguments to address, or examples to use — the closer the first draft will be to your final vision.

### The Compound Effect

For teams producing content at scale, the outline-first workflow compounds in value. Outlines can be reviewed and approved by editors before generation begins, keeping the AI aligned with editorial strategy across dozens or hundreds of articles. The result is a content operation where quality control happens at the structural level, not in post-production cleanup.
