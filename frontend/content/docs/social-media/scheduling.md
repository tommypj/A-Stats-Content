## How Scheduling Works

Instead of posting immediately, A-Stats lets you compose a post and set a future date and time for publication. Scheduled posts are stored in the A-Stats system and published automatically when the scheduled time arrives — no need to be logged in or have the app open.

The scheduler runs on A-Stats's servers, not in your browser, so posts are published reliably even if your device is offline or turned off at the scheduled time.

## Scheduling a Post

### Open the Composer

Start a post from **Social Media → Compose** or from the **New Post** button in the Social Calendar.

### Write Your Content

Write your post content, select the target platforms, and add any images as you would for an immediate post. See the Composing Posts guide for full details on the compose interface.

### Set the Schedule

Instead of clicking **Post Now**, click **Schedule**. A date and time picker will appear.

1. Select the date using the calendar picker.
2. Set the time using the hour and minute fields.
3. Confirm your timezone — A-Stats uses the timezone set in your account preferences. The picker displays local time based on that setting.

Click **Schedule Post** to save. The post will appear in your Social Calendar with a "Scheduled" status badge.

> Tip: Set your timezone in **Settings → Profile** before scheduling posts. If the timezone is wrong, all scheduled posts will fire at the wrong time.

## Optimal Posting Times

A-Stats does not currently provide automated "best time to post" suggestions, but general guidance based on platform research:

**Twitter / X**
- Weekdays between 8–10 AM and 12–1 PM in your audience's local timezone.
- Avoid posting late at night or on weekends for B2B topics.

**LinkedIn**
- Tuesday through Thursday, 7–9 AM or 12 PM. LinkedIn is a professional network with weekday-heavy activity.
- Avoid Fridays and weekends for most B2B content.

**Facebook**
- Wednesday around noon tends to perform well across industries.
- For consumer audiences, weekend mornings can be strong.

> Tip: The best posting time depends on your specific audience. After you have published 20–30 posts, review the engagement patterns in each platform's native analytics to identify when your audience is most active.

## Editing a Scheduled Post

Scheduled posts can be edited any time before they are published.

1. Open the **Social Calendar** and locate the scheduled post, or go to **Social Media → Scheduled** to see a list view.
2. Click the post to open the detail panel.
3. Click **Edit** to reopen the composer with the post content pre-filled.
4. Make your changes to the content, platform targets, image, or scheduled time.
5. Click **Update Schedule** to save the revised post.

> Note: Edits replace the entire post. The previous version is not retained after saving an edit. If you need to preserve the original wording, copy it before editing.

## Rescheduling to a Different Time

To move a scheduled post to a different time slot without editing the content:

1. Open the post from the calendar or scheduled list.
2. Click **Reschedule**.
3. Pick a new date and time.
4. Confirm with **Update**.

Alternatively, in the Social Calendar's week view, you can drag a scheduled post card to a different time slot directly on the calendar grid.

## Canceling a Scheduled Post

To remove a post from the schedule before it publishes:

1. Open the post from the calendar or scheduled list.
2. Click the three-dot menu and select **Cancel Post**.
3. Confirm the cancellation in the dialog.

Canceled posts are moved to a "Canceled" status in your post history and are not published. The content is retained in your history so you can reference it or recreate the post later if needed.

## What Happens if Publishing Fails

If A-Stats attempts to publish a scheduled post and the platform returns an error — for example, due to an expired OAuth token or a platform outage — the post is marked as **Failed** in your calendar.

You will receive an in-app notification when a scheduled post fails. To retry, open the failed post and click **Retry**. If the failure was due to a token expiry, reconnect the affected social account first, then retry.

Failed posts are not automatically retried on a delay. Manual retry is required to ensure you review the failure reason before republishing.
