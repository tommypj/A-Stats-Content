## The Social Calendar Overview

The Social Calendar gives you a visual overview of all your social media activity — past, present, and future — in a single calendar interface. Navigate to **Social Media → Calendar** from the sidebar to open it.

The calendar is the central hub for managing your social content schedule. Rather than scrolling through a flat list of posts, you can see at a glance how your publishing cadence looks across days and weeks, identify gaps where you have nothing scheduled, and spot days where you may be over-posting.

## Calendar Views

### Week View

The default view shows the current week across seven columns (one per day) with time slots running down the page. Scheduled posts appear as cards at their scheduled time within the appropriate day column.

Use the **Previous** and **Next** arrows at the top of the calendar to navigate backward and forward by one week at a time. Click **Today** to jump back to the current week.

Week view is best for managing your near-term publishing schedule and making sure the coming days are covered.

### Month View

Click the **Month** button in the view switcher to see a full month at a glance. Each day cell shows a count of posts scheduled for that day and a condensed list of post titles or platform icons.

Month view is better for spotting longer-term gaps and planning campaign cadence around events, product launches, or editorial themes.

> Tip: Most social media practitioners recommend a consistent publishing cadence over bursts of posts followed by silence. The month view makes it easy to see whether you are publishing consistently or erratically.

## Post Status Indicators

Posts on the calendar are color-coded by status so you can distinguish them at a glance:

- **Blue** — Scheduled and pending publication at the set time.
- **Green** — Published successfully.
- **Red** — Failed to publish. Requires attention.
- **Gray** — Canceled.

Platform icons on each post card indicate which social network the post targets. A post card with multiple platform icons was published or scheduled to multiple platforms simultaneously.

## Filtering the Calendar

The filter bar above the calendar lets you narrow what is displayed:

### Filter by Platform

Click the platform toggle buttons (Twitter/X, LinkedIn, Facebook) to show or hide posts for specific platforms. This is useful when you want to focus on LinkedIn scheduling alone, for example, without seeing all your Twitter posts cluttering the view.

### Filter by Status

Use the status filter to show only Scheduled, Published, Failed, or Canceled posts. The most useful combination for day-to-day management is Scheduled + Failed so you can see what is coming up and what needs attention.

## Viewing Post Details

Click any post card on the calendar to open the detail panel on the right side of the screen. The panel shows:

- The full post content as it was composed.
- The target platform(s).
- The scheduled or published time.
- The current status.
- Any error message if the post failed.
- Action buttons: **Edit**, **Reschedule**, **Cancel**, or **Retry** (for failed posts).

For published posts, the detail panel also shows a link to view the live post on the platform.

## Drag-and-Drop Rescheduling

In week view, you can drag a scheduled post card from one time slot to another to reschedule it without opening the edit interface. Click and hold the post card, then drag it to the new day and time slot. Release to drop it.

A confirmation tooltip will show the new scheduled time as you drag. After dropping, the schedule is updated automatically — no additional save step is needed.

> Note: Drag-and-drop rescheduling is only available for posts in "Scheduled" status. Published and canceled posts cannot be moved on the calendar.

## Creating Posts from the Calendar

You can create a new scheduled post directly from the calendar without going to the Compose screen. Click any empty time slot in week view or any empty day cell in month view to open a quick-compose panel pre-filled with the date and time you clicked.

This workflow is particularly efficient when you are blocking out content for the week — click each time slot you want to fill, write the post, and save without leaving the calendar view.

## Identifying and Filling Gaps

Days with no scheduled posts appear as empty columns in week view and empty cells in month view. Use these visual gaps to identify where your publishing cadence falls short.

> Tip: A common approach is to plan one week ahead. At the start of each week, open the calendar in month view, note the empty days in the coming 7–10 days, and fill them using the in-calendar quick-compose feature.

## Reviewing Past Performance from the Calendar

Published posts (shown in green) remain on the calendar indefinitely. Scrolling back through past weeks lets you review your publishing history and get a visual sense of volume and timing. For detailed engagement metrics on past posts, use the platform's native analytics — A-Stats does not currently import post-level engagement data (likes, shares, comments) from social platforms.
