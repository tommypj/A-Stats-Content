## The Compose Interface

The social post composer is accessible from two places: the **Social Media → Compose** item in the sidebar, and the **New Post** button available from the Social Calendar and the Connected Accounts page.

The composer is a unified interface where you write content once and target one or more connected platforms simultaneously. Platform-specific formatting rules and character limits are enforced live as you type.

## Selecting Target Platforms

At the top of the composer, select which platforms this post will be published to. You can select multiple platforms at once. Each selected platform appears as an active tab below the text editor, and the preview updates to show how the post will render on that platform.

If you do not have any accounts connected, the platform selector will prompt you to connect an account first.

> Tip: Even if you are posting to multiple platforms simultaneously, review each platform's preview tab before publishing. What reads well as a tweet may look too short on LinkedIn, where more context is expected.

## Writing Your Post

### Twitter / X

Twitter enforces a 280-character limit per post. A character counter appears at the bottom-right of the text editor and turns red when you are within 20 characters of the limit. Threads (multiple connected tweets) are not currently supported — each post is a single tweet.

Links count toward the character limit at their original length before being shortened by Twitter's t.co service. A-Stats shows you the raw character count.

### LinkedIn

LinkedIn allows up to 3,000 characters for personal profile posts and Company Page posts. There is no strict formatting syntax like Markdown — line breaks are respected but headings and bold text are not rendered from standard syntax.

To add emphasis on LinkedIn, use plain language phrasing rather than markdown syntax. Line breaks between short paragraphs tend to perform better than walls of text.

### Facebook

Facebook has a generous 63,206-character limit, though posts longer than a few hundred words see significantly lower organic reach. For best performance, keep Facebook posts concise and lead with the most engaging line since long posts are truncated behind a "See More" link.

## Platform-Specific Formatting Tips

**Hashtags**: All three platforms support hashtags. For Twitter, 1–2 hashtags per post is standard. For LinkedIn, 3–5 relevant hashtags placed at the end of the post tend to perform well. For Facebook, hashtags have minimal impact on reach and can be omitted.

**Mentions**: You can mention accounts using the `@username` syntax on Twitter and LinkedIn. Facebook mentions require the `@PageName` format and are more limited for non-personal accounts.

**Emojis**: Emojis are supported on all platforms and render correctly. They count toward character limits on Twitter.

## Adding Images

Click the **Add Image** button below the text editor to attach a photo. A-Stats supports JPEG, PNG, and GIF formats. Images are uploaded to your A-Stats media library and then attached to the post.

Platform-specific image constraints:
- **Twitter / X**: Up to 4 images per post. Recommended size: 1200×675 px (16:9 ratio).
- **LinkedIn**: 1 image for Company Page posts. Recommended size: 1200×627 px.
- **Facebook**: Up to 10 images per post. Recommended size: 1200×630 px.

> Tip: If you are posting to multiple platforms with different image aspect ratio requirements, upload the version that works best across all targets. Square images (1080×1080 px) tend to render acceptably on all three platforms.

## Previewing Before Posting

The **Preview** panel on the right side of the compose screen shows a simulated render of your post as it will appear on each selected platform. Toggle between platform tabs to check each one.

The preview is a close approximation — exact font rendering, link preview cards, and some platform UI details may differ from what users see on the actual platform.

## Posting Immediately

To publish the post right away, click **Post Now**. A-Stats sends the post to all selected platforms simultaneously. A success notification will appear confirming the post was published, along with a link to view it live on each platform.

If one platform fails (for example, due to a token expiry), A-Stats will report which platforms succeeded and which failed, and give you the option to retry the failed platform after reconnecting the account.

> Tip: After posting, the post appears in your Social Calendar with a "Published" status. You can view the original compose content and the platforms it was sent to from the calendar event detail panel.
